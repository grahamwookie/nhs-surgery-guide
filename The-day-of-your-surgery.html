Before your operation

Before your operation

You may have to wait. This depends on the number of operations before you and any emergencies.

Your nurse will help you change into a hospital gown and check things like your blood pressure.

Your anaesthetist will talk you through your anaesthetic.

Your surgeon will ask you to sign a consent form. They might mark the place on your body where they’re going to operate.

Bringing someone with you

Someone can come with you to the ward. While you get ready for surgery and have your operation they can wait in the waiting area or cafeteria.

If you’re having a general anaesthetic

You’ll be taken into the anaesthetic room, where the anaesthetist and their team will put you to sleep.

After your operation

You’ll go to a recovery room where you’ll be looked after by a nurse who will closely monitor you.

If you had general anaesthetic you may feel drowsy, sick, and have some pain - ask the nurse to give you medicine to help.

When you’ve recovered from the anaesthetic, you’ll be moved to another ward until you can go home.

The doctor will usually tell you before the surgery if you can go home the same or have a short hospital stay. Make sure you’ve arranged for someone to take you home.
